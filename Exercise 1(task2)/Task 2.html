Albert Einstein

About Albert Einstein

1879 — 1955

Albert Einstein was a German-born theoretical physicist who developed the theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics. He is best known for his equation E = mc², which has become the most famous equation in the world. His work reshaped our understanding of space, time, and energy.

Important Events

  1. 1879 — Born in Ulm, Germany.
  2. 1905 — Published the Annus Mirabilis papers, including special relativity.
  3. 1921 — Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics.
  4. 1933 — Moved to the United States, joined Princeton University.
  5. 1939 — Co-signed a letter to Roosevelt about nuclear research.
  6. 1955 — Passed away in Princeton, New Jersey.

Key Contributions

  • Theory of Special Relativity
  • Theory of General Relativity
  • Photoelectric Effect
  • Contributions to Quantum Mechanics
  • Mass–Energy Equivalence (E=mc²)
